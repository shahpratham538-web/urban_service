CHAPTER 4 SYSTEM ANALYSIS

4.1 Study of Current System
During my internship at Grownited Web Development Company, I studied the existing methods of providing and booking home services and identified several limitations.
• Manual and Unorganized Management:
Most residents still rely on manual processes such as individual phone calls, verbal communication, or searching through local directories for service providers (plumbers, cleaners, electricians). This leads to poor record management and difficulty in tracking service history.
• Lack of Centralized Platform:
There is no unified digital system where service seekers (residents) and service providers can interact seamlessly. Information about categories, pricing, and provider availability is often scattered, making it hard to compare and book services efficiently.
• Inefficient Booking and Scheduling:
Service requests are not tracked properly. There is no structured system to monitor booking status, ensure provider availability, or prevent scheduling conflicts. Residents often face delays because they don't know if a provider is actually available.
• Limited Communication and Feedback:
Communication between residents and service providers is not streamlined. Feedback or reviews are rarely documented, making it difficult for residents to identify high-quality providers and for providers to improve their service.
• Time-Consuming Processes:
Finding a reliable provider, negotiating prices, and confirming appointments require significant manual effort, consuming time and reducing overall convenience.
• Limited Use of Modern Technology:
Traditional service hiring does not utilize modern web applications, real-time notifications, or automated scheduling tools, which could significantly enhance the experience for both users and providers.

Conclusion:
The current service booking system is inefficient, transparent-lacking, and lacks digital integration. There is a strong need for a smart, centralized, and technology-driven Urban Service Platform to improve booking efficiency, transparency, and user experience.

4.2 Problem and Weakness of Current System
• Pricing and Data Inaccuracy:
Without a centralized platform, service charges vary significantly and are often not transparent. Incorrect service details or payment records can lead to confusion and disputes between residents and providers.
• Poor User Experience:
Existing fragmented methods are often not user-friendly. Residents face difficulty in finding verified providers across different categories like plumbing, electrical, or cleaning in one place.
• No Real-Time Updates:
Residents are not instantly notified about the progress of their booking, such as when a provider accepts a request, starts the work, or completes the service.
• Trust and Security Issues:
There are limited mechanisms to verify or rate service providers. Sensitive data like booking history and payment information may not be properly protected or documented.
• Lack of Automation:
Processes like scheduling, payment tracking, and status management are not automated, increasing the burden on residents to follow up manually.
• Limited Features:
Important features such as online payment systems, role-based dashboards (Resident, Provider, Support), service tracking, and structured reviews are missing in the traditional model.

4.3 Requirements of New System
The proposed Urban Service Platform is designed to overcome the limitations of the current manual service hiring systems.

• User-Friendly Dashboard:
The system should have a simple and intuitive interface so that residents, service providers, and support staff can easily navigate their respective sections without confusion.
• Modern and Responsive Design:
The platform must be fully responsive, utilizing Modern CSS techniques, to ensure it is easily accessible on smartphones for quick service browsing, booking, and notification tracking.
• Accurate and Real-Time Service Data:
The system must ensure that service categories, pricing, and provider profiles are updated instantly to provide reliable information to all users.
• Automated Scheduling & Conflict Prevention:
The system uses robust backend logic (Django ORM) and the ProviderAvailability model to automatically check time slots, preventing overlapping bookings for the same provider.
• Secure Authentication & Role-Based Access:
Secure login systems and Django authentication mechanisms are implemented to ensure user data privacy. The system strictly isolates Admin, Resident, Provider, and Support views to prevent unauthorized access.
• Structured Support & Ticketing System:
Users should be able to raise, categorize (e.g., billing, service quality), and track support tickets directly through the platform, ensuring no issue remains unresolved.
• Dynamic Notification Alerts:
The system features built-in real-time notifications for every status change in a booking (Pending to Accepted, Accepted to In Progress, etc.), keeping both residents and providers updated.
• Financial Tracking & Payments:
The system automates the tracking of service fees and payment statuses (Pending, Completed, Refunded), replacing manual record-keeping with a secure, centralized ledger.
• Verified Reviews & Ratings:
Residents can leave 1-5 star ratings and detailed feedback after service completion, helping build a trusted ecosystem of reliable service providers.
• Analytics and Reporting:
A centralized admin dashboard provides insights into platform metrics, including total services booked, provider performance, and unresolved support tickets, helping improve management decision-making.

4.4 System Feasibility

4.4.1 Does the System Contribute to the Overall Objectives of the Organization?
The proposed Urban Service Platform aligns with and supports the organization’s objectives by improving service efficiency and digital innovation.
• Improving Service Efficiency:
The system automates the processes of booking services, setting availability, and tracking payments. This eliminates manual bottlenecks and improves the speed of service delivery.
• Enhancing User Satisfaction:
By providing a modern interface with real-time updates and transparent reviews, the system ensures a smooth experience for residents and professional growth for providers.
• Reducing Manual Effort and Costs:
The platform eliminates the need for physical registers or multiple phone calls. The digital workflow reduces administrative effort and long-term operational costs.
• Improving Accuracy and Reliability:
With structured ORM-based storage and automated validation (like preventing booking overlaps), the system ensures high accuracy in record-keeping.
• Enhancing Technical Reputation:
Implementing a full-stack solution using Python and Django showcases the technical capability in building secure, data-driven web applications.

4.4.2 Can the System be Implemented Using the Current Technology?
Yes, the Urban Service Platform can be successfully implemented using widely used technologies within the given constraints.
• Use of Modern Technologies (Python, Django, CSS):
The system is developed using Modern Vanilla CSS for the frontend and Python with the Django framework for the backend. These are robust tools for secure and scalable applications.
• Responsive Frontend Development:
Modern CSS techniques (including Flexbox, Grid, and Glassmorphism effects) combined with Django templates ensure a highly accessible UI across devices.
• Django MTV Architecture:
Django’s Model-Template-View pattern splits database schemas, UI, and logic, making the system easier to maintain.
• Python Productivity:
Python’s clean syntax and extensive ecosystem allow for faster development of complex logic like scheduling and notifications.
• Django "Batteries-Included":
Essential features like role-based authentication and a built-in admin panel reduce the time required to build infrastructure.
• Django ORM:
The ORM provides a secure way to manage the database (SQLite/PostgreSQL) and prevents common security risks like SQL injection.
• Time and Resource Efficiency:
The use of open-source tools ensures minimal hardware costs, and Django’s efficiency enables completion within a standard 12-week development timeline.

4.4.3 Can the System be Integrated with Other Systems?
Yes, the system’s modular architecture and use of standard web technologies allow for easy integration.
• Modular Architecture:
Using independent apps for 'Core', 'Service', and 'Support' ensures that features can be added or integrated without disrupting the whole platform.
• API-Based Integration:
Django supports easy API generation to connect with external services like third-party email gateways, payment gateways (Razorpay/Stripe), or notification providers.
• Frontend Flexibility:
The template structure allows for integration with JavaScript libraries or external widgets seamlessly.
• Database Compatibility:
The ORM allows for smooth switching between development (SQLite) and production (PostgreSQL) databases, facilitating robust data management.

4.5 Activity / Process in New System
The following activities ensure the successful functioning of the Urban Service Platform:
• Requirement Gathering: Collecting needs from residents and providers to understand core features like booking, scheduling, and payments.
• System Design and UI Development: Designing intuitive dashboards for Residents and Providers using modern CSS and Django templates.
• Core Feature Development: Building logic for service categorization, provider-led availability management, and the full booking lifecycle.
• Booking & Conflict Management: Implementing backend logic to track booking statuses and prevent double-booking of providers.
• Payment Tracking: Developing segments for tracking service charges and payment confirmations against booking records.
• Support & Feedback System: Creating a ticketing module for issue resolution and a review module for provider ratings.
• Testing and Deployment: Rigorous testing for logic accuracy, role-based access, and mobile responsiveness before final deployment.

4.6 Features of New System
1. Centralized Service Marketplace: A unified platform for diverse service categories like plumbing, cleaning, and electrical works.
2. Smart Scheduling: Automated check of provider availability to ensure error-free booking dates.
3. Real-Time Status Tracking: Instant updates from booking request to completion.

4.7 List Main Modules of New System
• User Module: Manages profiles, role-based authentication (Resident, Provider, Admin, Support).
• Service & Category Module: Organizes services into industry categories with dynamic listings.
• Booking Module: Manages the lifecycle of service requests, status transitions, and scheduling.
• Availability Module: Allows providers to manage their working hours and time slots.
• Finance & Payment Module: Tracks service costs, payment IDs, and transaction statuses.
• Support Ticketing Module: Handles customer issues with priority-based tracking and resolution.
• Message & Notification Module: Powers internal communication between users and delivers system alerts.
• Review & Rating Module: Collects and displays resident feedback for service quality control.
• Admin Dashboard: Provides high-level analytics and control over all platform entities.
